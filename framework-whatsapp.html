Section 01

The complete customer flow

This is the end-to-end journey from the moment a customer messages you to the moment their order is confirmed and logged. Every step is either automated or reduced to a single action on your end.

Step 01
Customer sends first message
The customer messages your WhatsApp Business number for the first time. Before you even see it, the automated greeting message fires — presenting a simple menu of options. No manual response needed.
Tool: WhatsApp Business — Greeting Message
Step 02
Menu handles all FAQ automatically
The customer asks about prices, delivery, catalogue, or policy. Instead of you typing a response, a saved quick reply fires instantly. The customer gets a complete, professional answer in seconds.
Tool: WhatsApp Business — Quick Replies (12 templates)
Step 03
Ready to order — form link sent
When the customer says they want to order, a quick reply fires your Tally order form link. The form captures every detail you need — name, phone, items, quantity, pickup or delivery, location — without any back-and-forth.
Tool: Tally.so order form + /order quick reply
Step 04
Make.com receives the submission
The moment the form is submitted, Make.com catches it automatically. It formats the order, calculates the total including delivery fee, and fires back a structured WhatsApp message to the customer with the full order summary and payment details.
Tool: Make.com — Tally trigger + WhatsApp HTTP module + Sheets row
Step 05 — Your only manual step
You verify payment
The customer pays and sends their proof. You check your bank SMS or app, confirm the amount matches, and send one quick reply: /confirm. That single action triggers the next automated sequence.
Human step — estimated 30 seconds per order
Step 06
Three things happen simultaneously
Once you confirm: the customer gets an order confirmation message with their ETA. The order row in Google Sheets updates from Pending to Paid. The inventory count for the items sold decrements automatically. No manual logging.
Tool: Make.com — 3-branch scenario from /confirm trigger
Section 02

The 12 quick replies to set up

Copy these into WhatsApp Business under Settings → Quick Replies. Use the shortcut to fire the full message with a single keystroke.

How quick replies work: Type the shortcut (e.g. /cat) in any WhatsApp chat and the full saved message fires instantly. No typing. No copying. One tap sends a complete, professional response.

Shortcut When to use What it sends
/cat Customer asks what's available Full catalogue link + price range overview
/price Customer asks about prices Price ranges + ask for budget to filter
/order Customer ready to order Tally form link + what to fill in
/pay After customer confirms order Bank name, account number, account name
/confirm After you verify payment Order confirmed + delivery ETA
/del Customer asks about delivery Zones, fees, estimated time
/gift Customer asks about gift packaging Yes/no + price + upsell
/rec Customer wants a recommendation Ask budget + gender + vibe to personalise
/policy Customer asks about refunds or exchanges Full policy — clean and professional
/avail Customer asks if specific item is in stock "Checking now" — then confirm manually
/hours Customer texts outside business hours Working hours + when to expect a reply
/thanks After delivery is complete Thank you message + invite to return
Section 03

The Tally order form — field by field

Build this form at tally.so. It takes under 30 minutes to set up. Once live, every order arrives structured and complete — no more back-and-forth to collect basic information.

01
Full Name
Short text — Required
Used in the confirmation message and order log. Keep it simple.
Required
02
WhatsApp Number
Phone number — Required
Make.com uses this to send the automated confirmation message back to the customer.
Required
03
What would you like to order?
Long text — Required
Free text works better than dropdowns here — your catalogue is too large. Customer types the product name(s) and quantity. Example: "Yara Spray x2, Confetti Oil x1"
Required
04
Pickup or Delivery?
Multiple choice — Required
Options: "Self-pickup (Free)" / "Delivery (fees apply)". This controls whether the next field appears.
Required
05
Delivery Location
Multiple choice — Conditional
Only shows if "Delivery" is selected. Options: your exact delivery zones with fees shown next to each. Example: "Within Campus (₦800)" / "Asherrifa/Mayfair (₦1,200)"
Conditional
06
Gift packaging?
Multiple choice — Optional
Options: "Yes — add gift bag (₦1,500 extra)" / "No thanks". This is your upsell moment. Putting it in the form converts better than mentioning it separately.
Optional
07
Anything else we should know?
Long text — Optional
Catches special instructions, allergies, personal messages for gift orders, or anything the form fields did not cover.
Optional

Tally settings to configure: Under form settings, enable email notifications to yourself so every submission hits your inbox even before Make.com processes it. Set the form's thank you message to: "Your order is received. We will send your summary and payment details on WhatsApp within a few minutes."

Section 04

The Make.com scenario — module by module

This is the automation backbone. Build this scenario in Make.com using the free tier — 1,000 operations per month is enough for most small businesses starting out.

TRIGGER
Tally — Watch Responses
Watches your Tally form for new submissions. Every time someone submits the order form, this trigger fires and passes all the field values to the next modules.
ACTION
Tools — Set Variables
Calculate the order total: item prices + delivery fee (based on location field) + gift bag if selected (₦1,500). Store as a variable for use in the message module.
ACTION
HTTP — Send WhatsApp Message (via WhatsApp Cloud API or CallMeBot)
Sends a formatted message to the customer's WhatsApp number captured in the form. Message includes: order summary, total, payment details (bank + account number + name), and a deadline note.
ACTION
Google Sheets — Add a Row
Adds a new row to your Order Log sheet with: date, customer name, phone, items ordered, delivery zone, delivery fee, gift bag, total, and payment status set to "Pending".
ACTION
Gmail — Send Email to You
Optional but useful: sends yourself an email summary of every new order as a backup notification. Subject line: "New Order — [Customer Name] — [Total]"

Free tier note: Make.com's free plan gives you 1,000 operations per month and 2 active scenarios. This scenario uses 4 operations per order submission. At 10 orders per day, you will use roughly 1,200 operations per month — just above the free limit. Either upgrade to the Core plan (around $9/month) or reduce the scenario to 3 modules by removing the Gmail notification.
